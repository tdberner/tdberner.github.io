title: 2-DOF Golfer Waldo - Servo-Controlled Linkages
description:  A "waldo" is an input device used to track a motion that can then be used to move an output that is usually similar in structure to the input. My waldo involved designing and building a 2-degree-of-freedom planar mechanism to roughly replicate a golfer swinging at a ball. linkage system composed of a main body, an arm/shoulder link, and a golf club link. Two micro servos independently control the shoulder rotation and grip hinge joints, allowing coordinated swing motion.
skills: 
  - Laser-cut mechanical part design and fabrication
  - Analog-to-Digital sensor interfacing
  - Servo motor control
  - C programming for Atmega32u4
  - Breadboarding for circuit design
  - Mechanical design iteration and troubleshooting
